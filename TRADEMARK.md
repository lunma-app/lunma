# Lunma Trademark & Brand Policy

> **DRAFT.** A starting policy; have a lawyer review before the repository goes
> public. Securing the "Lunma" mark (or establishing common-law rights by being
> the first to publish) is a pre-launch task.

The Lunma **code** is open source under the Apache License 2.0 — you are free to
use, modify, and redistribute it. The Lunma **name, the "Lunma" wordmark, and the
logo** are different: they identify *this* project and its official builds, and
they are **not** licensed to you by the Apache License (see Apache 2.0 §6,
"Trademarks"). This page explains what's fine and what isn't.

## You may, without asking

- Use the Apache-2.0 code in your own projects, forks, and products.
- Say truthfully that your work is **"based on Lunma,"** **"a fork of Lunma,"** or
  **"compatible with Lunma,"** as a plain factual statement of origin.
- Refer to Lunma by name in articles, reviews, tutorials, and comparisons.

## Please do not, without written permission

- **Publish a fork or build under the name "Lunma"** (or a confusingly similar
  name) on the Chrome Web Store, Edge Add-ons, or anywhere else. Pick your own
  name for your distribution.
- **Use the Lunma name or logo** in a way that suggests your product, service, or
  distribution is the official Lunma, or is endorsed by or affiliated with the
  Lunma project — including in extension listings, app names, domains, social
  handles, or icons.
- **Modify the Lunma logo** or use it as the icon for a different product.

In short: **fork the code freely, but don't ship it as "Lunma."** The official
Lunma is the listing published by the Project Owner and linked from the official
site (`lunma.app`) and repository.

## Verifying the official build

The only official Lunma is the Chrome Web Store / Edge Add-ons listing linked from
`lunma.app` and this repository, published under the Project Owner's verified
developer account. When the listings are live, their extension IDs will be
documented on the site so you can confirm you have the real one.

## Reporting impersonation

If you find an extension or site impersonating Lunma or using the Lunma name/logo
without permission, please open an issue or contact the Project Owner so it can be
reported to the relevant store and, if necessary, addressed.

Questions about brand use that this page doesn't cover: contact the Project Owner.
